About me.

My name is Sean, I am en epidemiologist working for the UBC and BC Centres for Disease Control in Vancouver, British Columbia. My recent work focuses on COVID-19 variant severity.

My background includes various research roles related to health in academia, industry and volunteer positions, with a focus in methodology, data / statistical analysis and academic writing. I have a passion for research in health sciences as well as data science, specifically data analysis and data visualisaiton.

Experience


I currently conduct research related to COVID-19 variant of convern (VOC) severity at the BC Centre for Disease Control (BCCDC). Our work is focused on understanding how SARS-CoV-2 variants of concern impact disease severity and health outcomes using large administrative databases. We are also using SARS-CoV-2 sequencing data to evaluate the impact of other emerging variants.

My role is on focused on design and methodology of research and surveillance projects, data and statistical analysis, and scientific writing. This includes:

  • Conceptualising and conducting all aspects of epidemiologic research studies (i.e. hypotheses, study population, methodology, analysis, etc.)

  • Leading the statistical analysis in R: this includes exploratory analyses as well as creating data analysis pipelines from tidying raw data, applying a causal framework to analysis and to modelling severity outcomes

  • Leading the scientific writing of manuscripts for internal use and for publication in scientific journals

Our research focused in occupational health and safety oh health workers, specifically in relation to COVID-19. This work is a part of the WHO Collaborating Centre at UBC and lead to a publication in the BMJ Open (first author). Our research group also worked with Vancouver Coastal Health (VCH) to set up and run the Physician Occupational Health and Safety (POSH) Unit in Vancouver Coastal Health. Our goal was to fill the primary, secondary and tertiary prevention gap for physicians.

  • WHO Collaborating Centre: I co-lead a study in collaboration with the World Health Organization (WHO) which surveyed health care workers from around world about health risk and protection measures in the workplace during the pandemic.

  • POSH: Created a database of all physicians and collect data on exposures and test results to monitor physicians and other healthcare workers in Vancouver throughout the pandemic.

Our research group focused on studying how different factors of the urban environment impact cancer, physical activity and obesity. We used British Columbia’s largest cohort, the British Columbia Generations Project, to study epidemiological links between how different measures of the urban environment, like walkability, green space and air pollution impact cancer risks and health. We also studied how the built environment can affect cancer-causing behaviours. My role as a research assistant was to conduct literature reviews and research in the field, and contribute to the statistical analysis and writing academic papers.

  • Conducting analyses on a large prospective cohort

  • Scientific writing, including abstracts, literature reviews, reports and manuscripts

  • Completion of my Master's Thesis on breast cancer risk relatied to the built environement in post-menopausal women.

During my time as a research associate at Broadstreet Health Economic and Outcomes Research Broadstreet I gained in-depth experiences in the design and execution of various types of epidemiological studies with a focus on systematic reviews and meta-analyses. My research experience included co-leading a systematic review on lysosomal acid lipase deficiency and have co-authored a meta-analysis out for publication in the Journal of Managed Care Pharmacy.

  • Conducting a systematic literature review and network meta-analysis from strat to end, including abstract review and data extraction

  • Participation in extermal with pharmaceutical clients in relation to the research projects

Laurel L. Watters Research Fellowship

Award for MSc Thesis (2020)
A fellowship has been made available through an endowment established by the Estate of John Imrie Watters for a deserving graduate student or medical resident in the Faculty of Medicine engaged in research into the causes, treatment, and cure of breast cancer. Mr. Watters passed away in 2002. The awards are made on the recommendation of the Faculty of Medicine, and in the case of graduate students, in consultation with the Faculty of Graduate and Postdoctoral Studies.

Volunteer projects

Oasis Community Health Foundation (OCHF)

Project Lead for Chronic Disease Management Unit

2021 - present

OCHF is a small non-for-profit foundation which has been running a Malaria control and prevention program in rural Nigeria and has recently opened a new free rural health centre , in area with no nearby hospital. My role has been in helping organise the opening of the health centre and setting up the chronic disease division by creating clinical guidelines for providers and educational tools for patients. For more information on on OCHF, visit our website.

UBC Bhutan Health Project - Global Health Initiative

Research Study
Team Member

2021 - present

The goal of this initiative is to better understand the dynamics of youth mental health in Bhutan. This project in in partnership with a medical school in Bhutan and UBC department of Medicine. My role has been in been in writing the research protocol, applying for research ethics and funding as well as in the creation of a research survey for students in Bhutan to better understand mental health needs. I have also been leading the analysis of the research project. For more information on this project, visit our website.
