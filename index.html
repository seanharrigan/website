About me.

My name is Sean, and I am a Research Statistician at RainCity Analytics, a health outcomes and economics research company based in Vancouver, Canada. I lead projects focused on real-world evidence, systematic literature reviews and meta-analyses, and statistical analysis for safety and effectiveness outcomes, as well as regulatory and health technology assessment submissions across a range of disease areas, including cancer and rare diseases.

My background spans academia, public health, and industry, with roles at the BC Centre for Disease Control, UBC, and various global health initiatives. I have a passion for health research, epidemiology, statistical methods, and reproducible data science—particularly in data analysis, visualisation, and automation.

Experience


RainCity Analytics specialises in health economics and outcomes research across a diverse range of disease areas. As a Research Statistician, I work with a variety of clients, including pharmaceutical companies and global health organizations such as the WHO. My role spans various methodologies, from conducting systematic literature reviews (SLRs) and network meta-analyses (NMAs) to exploratory research in experimental and observational studies. In addition to leading the design and implementation of analytical frameworks, I am involved in academic writing of abstracts, reports, and manuscripts, and have served as the project lead for both systematic literature reviews and observational research studies.

Some of my key responsibilities include:

  • Lead and contribute to SLRs, meta-analyses, and observational research across a range of disease areas including oncology and rare diseases
  • Serve as project lead on multiple research studies, managing timelines, deliverables, and client communication
  • Design and implement analytical frameworks for real-world evidence (RWE) generation and health outcomes research
  • Conduct statistical analyses in R, including meta-analysis, survival analysis, and modelling of clinical outcomes
  • Author and contribute to scientific manuscripts, abstracts, and regulatory reports for HTA and peer-reviewed publication

I conducted research related to COVID-19 variants of concern (VOC) severity at the BC Centre for Disease Control (BCCDC). Our work used large administrative databases to understand how these VOCs impact disease severity. Specifically, my projects focused on VOC severity and how social determinants of health (e.g., income, education) can affect disease severity.

My role focuseded on the design and methodology of research projects, data and statistical analysis, and scientific writing. This included:

  • Conceptualising and conducting all aspects of epidemiologic research studies (i.e. hypotheses, study population, methodology, analysis, etc.)

  • Leading the statistical analysis in R: this includes exploratory analyses as well as creating data analysis pipelines from tidying raw data, applying a causal framework to analysis and modelling severity by using more complex analyses like propensity scores, inverse probability treatment weights and mediaion techniques

  • Leading the scientific writing of manuscripts for internal use and for publication in scientific journals

Our research was concentrated in occupational health and safety oh health workers, specifically in relation to COVID-19. During this role, I worked on two main projects: Physician Occupational Health and Safety (POSH) Unit in Vancouver Coastal Health and with the WHO Collaborating Centre at UBC.

POSH focused on providing a primary surveillance database for all physicans in Vancouver at the start of the pandemic. The was the first of its kind in the city and helped keep doctors safe during the beginning stages of the pandemic. The WHO collaborating Centre project was more focused on research. We collaborated with WHO teams in Siwtzerland to analyse a study of international healthworkers to better understand their perception of occupational safety during the pandmic.

  • WHO Collaborating Centre:
    • I co-lead a study in collaboration with the World Health Organization (WHO) which surveyed health care workers from around world about health risk and protection measures in the workplace during the pandemic, and understood how this was related to econimc indicators.
    • Conceptualisaiton of study and perfomed data analysis
    • Collaboration with international team members

  • POSH: Created a database of all physicians to collect data on COVID-19 exposures and test results to monitor physicians/healthcare workers in Vancouver throughout the pandemic.
    • Automated daily surveillance data from mlutiple hospitals to streamline data collection
    • Created weekly reports summarising COVID-19 infections and symptoms in the physcian population in Vancouver

Our research group studied how different characteristics of the urban environment may be associated with cancer risk, physical activity and obesity. We used British Columbia’s largest cohort, the British Columbia Generations Project, to study epidemiological links between how different measures of the urban environment, like walkability, green space and air pollution impact cancer risks and health. We also studied how the built environment can affect cancer-causing behaviours.

My role as a research assistant was to conduct literature reviews and research in the field, and contribute to the statistical analysis and writing academic papers.

  • Conducting analyses on a large prospective cohort

  • Scientific writing, including abstracts, literature reviews, reports and manuscripts

  • Completion of my Master's Thesis which analysed the association between breast cancer risk related to the built environement (namely, traffic-related air pollution, residential greenness and walkability) in post-menopausal women. My thesis was recognised by a number of significant awards listed in the recognition seciton below.

During my time as a research associate at Broadstreet Health Economic and Outcomes Research, I gained in-depth experience in the design and execution of various types of epidemiological studies with a focus on systematic reviews and meta-analyses. My research experience included co-leading a systematic review on lysosomal acid lipase deficiency and have co-authored a meta-analysis published in the Journal of Managed Care Pharmacy.

  • Conducting a systematic literature review and network meta-analysis from beginning to end, including abstract review and data extraction

  • Active participation in team meetings with pharmaceutical clients in relation to the research projects

  • Since this position, I have continued my education in the feild of HEOR, taking short courses and certificates in:

    • Decision modeling for Health Economic Evaulation
    • Meta-anlaysis concepts and analysis

Project Lead for Chronic Disease Management Unit

2021 - present

OCHF is a small non-for-profit foundation which has been running a Malaria control and prevention program in rural Nigeria and has recently opened a new free rural health centre, in area with no nearby hospital. My role has been in helping organise the opening of the health centre and setting up the chronic disease division by creating clinical guidelines for providers and educational tools for patients.
